About CBK Project

FROM ADVERSITY
TO ADVOCACY

Every comeback starts with someone who refused to give up. This is the story of how the Comeback Kid Project came to be — and why we're not going anywhere.

WHY WE STARTED

Utah has thousands of young people aging out of foster care, battling addiction, and rebuilding after trauma — with no map and no one walking beside them.

The Comeback Kid Project was founded in 2025 in Lehi, Utah by Michael Walker — a man who knows what it's like to fight back from rock bottom. Michael battled addiction as a teenager and young adult, and had to write his own comeback story without a playbook. That experience lit a fire that never went out.

His belief is simple: every young person who has faced adversity deserves someone in their corner. Not a handout. Not a program that checks a box. A real person who shows up, stays, and walks beside them. CBK Project is the thing Michael wished existed when he was the one who needed it most.

We are a registered 501(c)(3) public charity verified by the IRS, meaning every dollar donated is tax-deductible and goes directly to serving youth.

WHAT MAKES US DIFFERENT

We don't fix people — we walk with them while they rebuild. Our trauma-informed approach means we understand what these young people have been through, and we meet them where they are.

Based at the foot of Utah's Wasatch Mountains, we believe that just like those peaks, young people are capable of rising to extraordinary heights when given the right foundation.

OUR VALUES

Three core values drive everything we do. They're not just words on a wall — they're the standard we hold ourselves to every single day.

Integrity

We are who we say we are, regardless of who is around.

Accountability

We keep our commitments. We take responsibility for our results.

Service

Healing comes through lifting others.

THE FOUNDER

Michael Walker didn't start the Comeback Kid Project from a boardroom — he started it from experience.

As a teenager and young man, Michael fought his own battles with addiction and adversity. There was no roadmap. No mentor waiting on the other side. He had to claw his way back — and he did. That fight, and everything it taught him, became the foundation for everything CBK Project stands for.

Today, Michael is a Pediatric Psychiatric Nurse, a father in a blended family of five children, and an avid skateboarder who still believes the best way to clear your head is four wheels and concrete. He brings the same grit to this organization that carried him through his own comeback.

He is the Comeback Kid. And now he's building the thing he wished existed when he needed it most — a community of real people who show up, stay, and walk beside young people while they write their own next chapter.
